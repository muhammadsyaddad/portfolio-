## Spectral Decomposition

Let $A \in \mathbb{R}^{n \times n}$ be symmetric. Then

$$
A = Q\Lambda Q^\top, \quad Q^\top Q = I, \quad \Lambda = \mathrm{diag}(\lambda_1,\dots,\lambda_n).
$$

The Rayleigh quotient

$$
\rho_A(x) = \frac{x^\top A x}{x^\top x}
$$

achieves its extrema at eigenvectors of $A$.

## Variational Derivation

Consider the constrained problem

$$
\min_{x \in \mathbb{R}^n} \; x^\top A x \quad \text{s.t.} \quad \|x\|_2^2 = 1.
$$

Lagrangian:

$$
\mathcal{L}(x,\lambda) = x^\top A x - \lambda (x^\top x - 1).
$$

Stationarity gives

$$
\nabla_x \mathcal{L} = 2Ax - 2\lambda x = 0 \Rightarrow Ax = \lambda x.
$$

## Matrix Identities

The Woodbury identity:

$$
(A + UCV)^{-1} = A^{-1} - A^{-1}U(C^{-1} + VA^{-1}U)^{-1}VA^{-1}.
$$

The determinant lemma:

$$
\det(A + uv^\top) = (1 + v^\top A^{-1}u)\det(A).
$$

## Multiline Alignment

$$
\begin{aligned}
\|Ax - b\|_2^2
&= (Ax - b)^\top (Ax - b) \\
&= x^\top A^\top A x - 2 b^\top A x + b^\top b.
\end{aligned}
$$

## Probability and Information

Let $p$ and $q$ be distributions on $\mathcal{X}$. The KL divergence is

$$
D_{\mathrm{KL}}(p\|q) = \int_{\mathcal{X}} p(x) \log\frac{p(x)}{q(x)} \, dx.
$$

For a Gaussian $\mathcal{N}(\mu,\Sigma)$, the log-likelihood of data $\{x_i\}_{i=1}^m$ is

$$
\log p(X) = -\frac{m}{2}\log\det(2\pi\Sigma) - \frac{1}{2}\sum_{i=1}^m (x_i-\mu)^\top \Sigma^{-1}(x_i-\mu).
$$

## PDE Example

Heat equation with initial condition:

$$
\begin{cases}
\partial_t u = \alpha \Delta u, & x \in \Omega, \; t>0, \\
u(x,0) = u_0(x), & x \in \Omega.
\end{cases}
$$

The solution in Fourier space is

$$
\hat{u}(k,t) = e^{-\alpha \|k\|_2^2 t} \hat{u}_0(k).
$$

## Optimization

Newton step for $f: \mathbb{R}^n \to \mathbb{R}$:

$$
x_{t+1} = x_t - \left(\nabla^2 f(x_t)\right)^{-1}\nabla f(x_t).
$$

For constrained optimization with equality constraints $g(x)=0$:

$$
\nabla f(x) + J_g(x)^\top \lambda = 0, \quad g(x)=0.
$$

## Complex Analysis

Residue theorem (for isolated singularities $z_k$ inside $\gamma$):

$$
\oint_{\gamma} f(z)\,dz = 2\pi i \sum_k \mathrm{Res}(f; z_k).
$$
